I am a researcher in Computational Materials Science with a Masters degree in Physics from Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Delhi. I have worked on the theoretical formulation of carbon-based two-dimensional (2D) materials like graphene and their quasi-one-dimensional (1D) nanoribbon heterostructures. Prior to that, I was actively involved in neutrino detector simulation studies of the Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment (DUNE) at Fermi Lab, United States. Besides research in the basic sciences, I am keenly interested in teaching high school/undergraduate physics and mathematics.

 My current research problems have led me to develop an interest in machine learning and artificial intelligence. This has motivated me to enroll in the data science diploma program at Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Madras, through which I hope to augment my data analysis skills and correctly draw inferences to solve data-driven problems in industry and academia.
